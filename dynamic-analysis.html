Dynamic Analysis in FEM

Introduction

Dynamic analysis in FEM is used to study how structures respond to time-dependent forces, such as vibrations, impacts, and seismic activities.

Types of Dynamic Analysis

  1. Modal Analysis: Determines the natural frequencies and mode shapes of a structure.
  2. Harmonic Analysis: Studies the response of a structure to sinusoidal time-dependent loads.
  3. Transient Analysis: Analyzes the response of a structure to time-varying loads over a period.
  4. Random Vibration Analysis: Used for systems subjected to random, stochastic forces.

Example: Modal Analysis

The eigenvalue problem for a vibrating system is represented as:

        (K - λM) * Φ = 0

Where K is the stiffness matrix, M is the mass matrix, λ is the eigenvalue (related to the square of the natural frequency), and Φ is the mode shape vector.

Solution Techniques

  1. Direct Integration: Numerical methods like Newmark’s method or the central difference method to integrate the equations of motion.
  2. Frequency Domain Analysis: Used in harmonic analysis to solve for the steady-state response using frequency response functions.

Summary

Dynamic analysis provides insights into how structures behave under various dynamic conditions, crucial for designing structures to withstand real-world forces and vibrations.
