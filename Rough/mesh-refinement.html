Mesh Refinement in FEM

Introduction

Mesh refinement is the process of adjusting the mesh in FEM to improve the accuracy of the solution. Finer meshes lead to more accurate results but require more computational resources.

Why Refine the Mesh?

  1. Accuracy: To capture detailed behavior in areas with high gradients.
  2. Convergence: To ensure that the solution converges to the true value as the mesh is refined.
  3. Error Estimation: To estimate the discretization error and ensure the solution is within acceptable limits.

Mesh Refinement Techniques

  1. Uniform Refinement: Divide all elements into smaller elements.
  2. Adaptive Refinement: Refine the mesh in regions with high error or stress concentrations.
  3. Local Refinement: Refine specific regions of interest based on prior knowledge or analysis.

Example: Refining a 1D Bar Element

Consider a 1D bar divided into 4 elements. Refining the mesh may involve increasing the number of elements from 4 to 8:

        Original Mesh: [ 0 - 1 - 2 - 3 - 4 ]
        Refined Mesh:  [ 0 - 0.5 - 1 - 1.5 - 2 - 2.5 - 3 - 3.5 - 4 ]

Summary

Mesh refinement enhances the accuracy of FEM simulations by improving the resolution of the mesh in areas of interest or where high gradients are present.
