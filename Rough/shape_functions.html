Shape Functions: Theory and Applications

Introduction

Shape functions are used in FEM to interpolate the solution over the domain of an element. They play a critical role in defining how the field variables vary within the element.

Example: 1D Linear Shape Function

For a 1D linear element:

        N1 = 1 - ξ
        N2 = ξ

where ξ is the local coordinate within the element.

Applications

Shape functions are used to approximate displacements and stresses within the element, allowing for accurate simulation of structural behavior.

Summary

Shape functions are fundamental for the accurate representation of field variables in FEM and are essential for solving complex structural problems.
