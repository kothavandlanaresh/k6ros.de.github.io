Nonlinear Analysis in FEM

Introduction

Nonlinear analysis in FEM is used to model problems where the relationship between forces and displacements is not linear. This includes large deformations, nonlinear material behavior, and contact problems.

Types of Nonlinearities

  1. Geometric Nonlinearity: Occurs when deformations are large enough to affect the structure's stiffness. The equilibrium equations need to account for changes in the geometry.
  2. Material Nonlinearity: Arises when the material's stress-strain relationship is nonlinear. Examples include plasticity and hyperelasticity.
  3. Contact Nonlinearity: Occurs in problems where elements come into contact with each other, affecting the overall behavior.

Example: Nonlinear Material Behavior

For a material that follows a bilinear stress-strain curve, the stress-strain relationship is:

        σ = E1 * ε  for ε < εy
        σ = E1 * εy + E2 * (ε - εy)  for ε ≥ εy

Where σ is the stress, ε is the strain, E1 is the initial modulus, E2 is the modulus after yielding, and εy is the strain at yield.

Solution Techniques

  1. Incremental Loading: Apply loads in increments and solve the problem at each step.
  2. Newton-Raphson Method: An iterative method used to solve nonlinear equations by linearizing the problem at each iteration.

Summary

Nonlinear analysis is essential for accurately modeling complex behaviors in structural and mechanical systems where linear assumptions are insufficient.
