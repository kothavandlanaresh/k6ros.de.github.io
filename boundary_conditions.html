Applying Boundary Conditions in FEM

Introduction

Boundary conditions are essential for defining how a structure is constrained or loaded in FEM. They ensure that the model accurately reflects real-world constraints and loads.

Types of Boundary Conditions

  • Displacement Boundary Conditions: Specify the displacement of nodes.
  • Force Boundary Conditions: Apply forces or loads to nodes.

Example

Consider a fixed-end beam:

        u1 = 0  (fixed at one end)
        u2 = 0  (fixed at one end)

Applications

Boundary conditions are used to model real-world constraints and loading conditions, such as fixed supports, roller supports, and applied forces.

Summary

Correctly applying boundary conditions is crucial for accurate FEM simulations and ensuring realistic results.
