Boundary Conditions in FEM

Introduction

Boundary conditions are essential for solving FEM problems as they define how the model interacts with its environment. They are applied to the edges or boundaries of the problem domain.

Types of Boundary Conditions

  1. Dirichlet Boundary Conditions: These specify the value of the variable at the boundary. For example, specifying a fixed temperature or displacement.
    2.             u(x) = u0
  2. Neumann Boundary Conditions: These specify the derivative of the variable at the boundary, often representing fluxes or forces.
    3.             σ * n = t
  3. Robin Boundary Conditions: A combination of Dirichlet and Neumann conditions, specifying both the value and the gradient of the variable.
    4.             α * u + β * ∂u/∂n = g

Example: Fixed Support

For a fixed support in a structural analysis problem, the boundary condition would be:

        u = 0
        v = 0

Where u and v are the displacements in the x and y directions, respectively.

Summary

Applying the correct boundary conditions is crucial for obtaining accurate results in FEM analysis, as they represent the constraints and interactions of the model with its environment.
