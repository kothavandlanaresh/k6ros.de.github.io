Finite Element Method (FEM)

The Finite Element Method (FEM) is a powerful numerical method widely used in engineering to solve problems in structural analysis, fluid dynamics, heat transfer, and electromagnetics.

FEM breaks down complex problems into smaller, simpler parts, called finite elements, which are easier to solve.

FEM is especially useful when dealing with irregular geometries, material properties, and boundary conditions. It allows for highly accurate approximations and is employed in fields such as civil engineering, mechanical engineering, and aerodynamics. Learn more about the basics here
